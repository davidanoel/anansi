# Finding the Right Designer for Anansi — Sourcing Guide

The searches I ran didn't return actionable designer names — aggregators dominate those results, and the real talent is found by doing targeted manual browsing on the platforms where designers showcase their work. Here is the strategy that actually works.

---

## Strategy 1: Dribbble (Best for finding the specific aesthetic)

**URL:** https://dribbble.com

**Step 1:** Go to https://dribbble.com/search/logo-identity and sort by "Most Liked — This Year"

**Step 2:** Keep scrolling until you see marks that share DNA with what you want for Anansi. You'll recognize them immediately — they're the ones that look like ancient symbols rather than modern logos.

**Step 3:** Search these specific terms and bookmark designers whose portfolios resonate:

| Search term | What you're hunting for |
|---|---|
| `tribal mark` | Indigenous-rooted modern marks |
| `petroglyph logo` | Stone-carving aesthetic |
| `west african branding` | Diaspora-sensitive designers |
| `caribbean identity` | Regional specialists |
| `hieroglyph logo` | Ancient-symbol treatment |
| `monogram geometric` | Grid-based construction |
| `crest logo` | Crown/heraldic treatments |
| `minimal bird logo` / `minimal animal logo` | Clean figurative marks |

**Step 4:** On each designer's profile, check:
- Do they have a full identity system shown (not just one logo)?
- Do they show their grid/construction?
- Have they done brand guidelines PDFs?
- Is there a "Hire me" link with pricing or rates?

**Step 5:** Save 10-15 designers to a shortlist. Contact 5. You'll likely get responses from 2-3.

**Typical pricing range on Dribbble:** $1,500 – $8,000 depending on experience.

---

## Strategy 2: Working Not Working (Best for vetted senior talent)

**URL:** https://www.workingnotworking.com

This is where experienced designers who used to work at agencies now freelance. The bar is higher, the rates are higher, the quality is more consistent.

**How to use:**
1. Create an account (free for clients)
2. Post a job listing with the brief above
3. Browse "Brand Design" freelancers and message 5-10 directly

**Typical pricing:** $4,000 – $12,000 for this scope.

**Best for:** If you have budget and want minimal risk.

---

## Strategy 3: Behance + Direct Outreach (Best for cultural specificity)

**URL:** https://www.behance.net

**Step 1:** Search these terms:
- `indigenous brand identity`
- `afro-caribbean branding`
- `cultural logo design`
- `heritage brand identity`

**Step 2:** Filter by "Creative Fields: Branding" and "Tools: Adobe Illustrator" (ensures vector work, not illustration)

**Step 3:** For each promising portfolio, click through to the designer's profile. Look for:
- A visible "Hire Me" or "Contact" button
- Location (US-based or same timezone is easier)
- Recent projects (active in the last 6 months)

**Step 4:** Direct message through Behance or use the email they list.

**Typical pricing:** $2,000 – $7,000.

## Red Flags When Vetting

Ignore any designer who:

- Has no brand guidelines PDFs in their portfolio (suggests they've never delivered a full system)
- Only shows logos on clean white backgrounds (no in-context mockups)
- Charges by the hour for fixed-scope identity work (suggests inexperience pricing projects)
- Says they can deliver in under 2 weeks (rushing identity is how you get AI-tier work)
- Has no published rates or "contact for pricing" across every post (often means inconsistent pricing, possibly inexperience)
- Shows the same mark applied to many unrelated projects (template work)

---

## Green Flags

Prioritize designers who:

- Show the *construction* of their marks (grid overlays, development stages)
- Have case studies with client quotes and results
- List clear fixed-price packages
- Show brand guidelines PDFs in their portfolio
- Have Instagram or Twitter presence (suggests they care about craft)
- Have repeat clients (mentioned in testimonials)
- Show cultural sensitivity in how they talk about their work
- Respond to your email within 48 hours (responsiveness predicts process quality)

---

## What to Send When You Reach Out

Short, direct email. Designers are flooded with vague "can you make me a logo?" messages. Stand out with specificity.

**Subject:** `Brand identity redraw — $2.5-5K — Miami tech company (Anansi)`

**Body:**

> Hi [Designer],
>
> I found your work through [specific project they did]. The [specific aspect — e.g., "grid construction on the X mark" or "the cultural sensitivity of the Y rebrand"] is exactly what I'm looking for.
>
> I'm the founder of Anansi Technology Corporation, a Miami-based AI and RWA company rooted in Caribbean heritage. We have a strong concept mark that was generated by AI and iterated — the *concept* is solid, but the *craft* needs professional vector redraw. I want to hire someone to fix it properly.
>
> Full brief attached. Budget is $2,500-$5,000 for the deliverables listed. Timeline flexible but we'd like to start within 2-3 weeks.
>
> If this interests you, I'd love to book a 20-minute call to see if we're a fit.
>
> Best,
> [Your name]
> anansi.tech

Attach `anansi-logo-design-brief.md` (or PDF version) and the current `logo-clean.png`.

## Sequencing Recommendation

**Week 1:** Browse Dribbble, Behance, Working Not Working. Save 20 candidates. Narrow to 10.

**Week 2:** Send initial outreach to top 5. Have 3-4 intro calls.

**Week 3:** Pick the designer. Kick off the project.

**Weeks 4-7:** Design process (concepts → feedback → refinement → delivery).

**Week 8:** New logo is live. Ready for SAFT pitch, partnership meetings, and public launch.

---

## Final Note

The single most important signal when evaluating designers: **do they have opinions, or are they order-takers?** You want someone who will push back on the brief, challenge your assumptions, and make the mark *better* than what you asked for. Order-takers produce what you described. Opinion-havers produce what you needed.

Ask every designer in your intro call: "What do you think is wrong with our current logo?" Their answer tells you everything about how they will work.
