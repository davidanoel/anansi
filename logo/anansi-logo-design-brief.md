## The Current Mark

The existing logo was developed through iterative AI generation. The **concept** is strong and should be preserved. The **execution** has craft issues that we need a professional designer to resolve.

**What the mark represents:**

- **Anansi** — the spider-trickster from West African and Caribbean folklore, who outsmarts larger powers through cleverness rather than force; weaves webs that connect what was disconnected
- **A human figure** — the body of the spider reads as a human with raised arms, referencing indigenous Carib / Taíno petroglyphs found across the Caribbean islands
- **A red crown** — above the head of the figure, signifying sovereignty, leadership, and the elevation of the archetype
- **Eight legs** — radiating from a central body, suggesting both the spider and a web of connections

The mark should feel **mythological, architectural, and modern simultaneously** — like an ancient symbol that was always waiting to be used as a tech company logo.

**Reference attached:** `logo-current.png` (primary mark)

## The Craft Issues to Fix

The new designer should address these specific problems:

1. **Inconsistent leg terminals.** The outer legs have curved hook tips; the inner legs have squared-off rectangular ends. Pick one vocabulary (we prefer the curved / organic terminals) and apply it consistently across all eight legs.

2. **Uneven stroke weights.** The body, legs, and crown use three slightly different line weights. Normalize to a single consistent stroke weight across the entire mark, with optical adjustments where the eye requires them.

3. **Crown proportions.** The crown is slightly too thin relative to the body, and sits a touch too low — it should feel like it crowns the figure from above, not sits on the head. Recommend thickening the crown by ~25% and raising it slightly.

4. **Optical balance.** The mark reads as slightly bottom-heavy. The visual center of gravity should sit at the figure's heart/chest, not at the intersection of the legs.

5. **Small-size breakdown.** At sizes below ~40px, the eight-leg silhouette fills in and becomes visually noisy. We need a **simplified small-size variant** that preserves identity at favicon and nav scales.

## Deliverables

### Primary mark (full fidelity)
- Complete spider-human-with-crown figure
- For use at 80px and above
- Horizontal and stacked orientation

### Simplified mark (small-size variant)
- Reduced element count for legibility at small sizes
- For use under 40px (favicons, app icons, social avatars, in-line nav)
- Should preserve immediate identity with the primary mark
- Designer proposal welcome — could be crown + simplified figure, or stylized A-form with crown, or another approach

### Wordmark
- "ANANSI" in a typeface that complements the mark
- We currently use Satoshi (by Indian Type Foundry) for the wordmark — designer can propose an alternative or refine the existing treatment
- Letter spacing, weight, and tracking should feel architectural and deliberate

### Lockups
- Primary mark + wordmark, horizontal
- Primary mark + wordmark, stacked
- Simplified mark + wordmark, horizontal (compact)
- Wordmark only

### Color variants
- Full color (black body, red crown — primary)
- All black (for single-color light backgrounds)
- All white (for dark backgrounds)
- All red (for accent applications)
- Reverse / knockout variants

### File formats
- SVG (master files — preferred for all digital use)
- PNG @ 1x, 2x, 3x for raster use
- PDF for print
- AI or Figma source files for future editing
- Favicon set (16x16, 32x32, 48x48) as both ICO and PNG
- Apple touch icon and Android icon variants

### Brand guidelines document (10-15 page PDF)
- Logo anatomy and construction grid
- Clear-space rules and minimum sizes
- Approved color variants and usage
- Typography pairings (Satoshi for display, DM Sans for body — already defined)
- Color palette with Pantone, CMYK, RGB, Hex values
- Do's and don'ts with examples

## Tone and Feeling

**We want the mark to feel:**

- Mythological but modern
- Engineered but hand-carved
- Confident but not loud
- Culturally rooted but globally legible
- Architectural, like a symbol etched in stone
- Refined like Nike, Apple, or Hermès — not busy like a crypto project

**We do NOT want the mark to feel:**

- Cartoon-y or illustrated
- Generic Web3 (no gradients, no neon, no cyberpunk tropes)
- Overly tribal or "ethnic stock art"
- Corporate-generic (no floating swooshes)
- AI-generated (no soft edges, no rendered shadows, no gradient fills)

---

## Reference Neighborhood

Marks that live in the same visual neighborhood as what we want Anansi to become:

- **Apple** — restraint, single-weight, iconic at every size
- **Hermès** — heritage craft, hand-built feel, refined
- **Nike** — confident motion, instantly readable
- **Palantir** — minimal, serious, engineered
- **MSCHF** — cheeky but controlled execution
- **SpaceX** wordmark — slightly futurist, mechanically precise
- **Section 35** — modern indigenous streetwear, shows cultural rooting done right
